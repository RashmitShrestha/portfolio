Bermuda Triangle: The triangular area in the Atlantic Ocean bounded by Bermuda, Puerto Rico, and a point near Melbourne, Florida, in which a number of ships and aircraft have disappeared.

Microburst: An intense, downdraft of air that spreads on the ground,causing massive waves and turbulence.

Trench: A long, steep-sided, narrow depression in the ocean floor.

Methane: A colorless, odorless, flammable gas, CH 4, which can cause Global Warming.

Magnetism: The properties of attraction possessed by magnets; the molecular properties common to magnets.

Kraken: A legendary sea monster causing large whirlpools off the coast of Norway.

UFO: Any unidentified flying object observed in the sky, especially from extraterrestrial life.

Updraft: An upward current of draft of rising air.

Wormhole: A theoretical passageway in space between a black hole and a white hole.

Atlantis :A legendary and fictional island, supposedly have sunken in the Atlantic ocean. Atlantis was first mentioned by Plato.

G-Force: Force of Gravity.

Speed: the rate at which someone or something is able to move or operate.

GPS: A system of satellites and devices used to mark longitude and latitude to globally position a target. Or Global Positioning System.

EMP: An electromagnetic pulse which can be used to destroy susceptible electronic equipment over a wide area.

Turbulence: Unsteady movement due to different air pressures or water currents.

Hutchison Effect: Collections of phenomena that John Hutchison found out to study longitudinal waves.

Electromagnetic: Metal with coils wrapped around when adding a electrical current, turns magnetic.

Virga: Observable streak of precipitation falling from a cloud that evaporates before hitting the ground.

Precipitation: Condensation of water vapor that falls due to gravity.

Velocity: Speed

Eon: An indefinite and very long amount of time.

Paradox: A statement that leads to it making no sense.

Paranormal: Experiencing events that make no scientific sense.
